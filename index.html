About Me

Hi ! I Am Pranjali

I work as a Senior Software Engineer in the Securities Division of a French International Bank - Societe Generale. I hold a Bachelors of Engineering in Computer Science from Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra, a pioneer and premium college of Technology in India.

The Work I Do

I overlook the integration of multiple applications and their smooth communication. I gather requirements and add functionalities and transformational logic between upstream/downstream applications. I lead an innovation team and our primary goal is to strategise and improve efficiency of financial systems.

Sports

I have always been a sports enthusiast. As a kid, I used to play Badminton and partcipate in state-level competitions. I played Basketball all through my college years and was part of my college Basketball team. I believe Sports is a great way to keep our body and mind fit and to develop team spirit and sportsmanship.

Volunteering

As an advocate for leadership through community involvement, I actively work with 2 organisations- NSS and Hands4Scoiety. I also initiate and organise Société Générale's CSR initiatives. I have organised and mentored at Connexion - an event to integrate differentially abled people in to the workforce.

Leadership through Community

NSS and Me

I have been part of the National Service Scheme chapter since a long time. NSS played a big part in shaping my personality and bringing out the leadership skills in me. I have been a part of NSS for almost 3 years and in that course, I had the opportunity to meet and work with so many different and extremely talented people, organise a lot of events and interact with many students. Community service has taught me a great number of skills - you have to think on your feet, make decisions and collaborate with others. It has increased my confidence, instilled a sense of belonging and purpose, and more importantly, it has helped create joy.

Hands4Society - Together We Can.

I teach Maths, English and Behavioural skills to a group of children. An important aspect for the overall development of a child is the EQ - Emotional quotient. EQ is predominantly affected by the environment in which a child grows. Challenging and questioning the built in moral compass and providing space for something new and unique is my mission through this group.
